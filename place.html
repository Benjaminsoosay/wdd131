About Paris

Paris, the capital of France, is one of the most iconic and visited cities in the world. Known as the "City of Light" (La Ville Lumière), it's celebrated for its cultural richness, architectural beauty, and historical significance.

Founded in the 3rd century BC by a Celtic tribe called the Parisii, the city has evolved through centuries of history, from Roman conquests to royal reigns and revolutions.

Today, Paris stands as a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture, attracting millions of visitors each year to its famous landmarks, museums, and charming streets.

Must-See Attractions

Eiffel Tower

The iconic iron tower built by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 World's Fair, offering breathtaking views of the city.

Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum, home to the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and thousands of other masterpieces.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

A masterpiece of French Gothic architecture, dating back to the 12th century (currently under restoration).

Culture & Cuisine

Parisian culture is a blend of refined artistry and everyday charm. From world-class museums to cozy corner cafés, the city offers endless opportunities to experience the French "art de vivre" (art of living).

Paris is also a culinary capital, boasting everything from Michelin-starred restaurants to charming bistros and patisseries. Don't miss trying classic French dishes like coq au vin, croissants, escargots, and crème brûlée.

The city's arrondissements (districts) each have their own unique character, from the artistic vibe of Montmartre to the intellectual atmosphere of the Latin Quarter.
